## Acceptance of Postdoctoral Researchers and Research Fellows

We welcome applications from postdoctoral researchers and research fellows interested in conducting research using theoretical and computational science. Prospective applicants are encouraged to review the information on this website and publications from our laboratory to gain a solid understanding of our research activities. After doing so, please contact the  laboratory head, Dr. Yuji Sugita, by email to discuss potential opportunities.
(sugita_at_riken.jp — please replace “at” with “@”)

### Postdoctoral Researchers and Research Fellows Engaged in Research Projects

<p class="is-indent">We regularly recruit postdoctoral researchers (within five years of obtaining a Ph.D.) and research fellows (more than five years after the Ph.D.) to participate in ongoing research projects within our group. Unless limited by specific budgetary constraints, members are encouraged not only to contribute to project-based research but also to pursue their own original ideas by applying for funding opportunities such as JSPS KAKENHI grants or RIKEN Incentive Research Projects.
Many of our projects involve either the development of computational methods and models—often using platforms such as GENESIS—or the execution of large-scale simulations on supercomputers. For the former, proficiency in at least one programming language or parallel computing framework (e.g., Python, Fortran, C/C++, OpenMP, MPI, OpenACC, CUDA) is highly desirable. For the latter, experience with molecular dynamics simulations, as well as knowledge of multiscale simulations and free-energy calculation techniques, is expected.</p>

### RIKEN Special Postdoctoral Researcher Program (SPDR)

<p class="is-indent">This program provides an environment at RIKEN where young researchers can pursue their work independently and creatively. The SPDR program supports researchers in conducting their own proposed projects with a high degree of autonomy. Appointments are for a fixed three-year term, and applications are typically accepted from February to early April each year. Prospective applicants should contact the laboratory head well in advance. Candidates are selected through document screening and interviews conducted by a committee composed of researchers from both inside and outside RIKEN. For further details, please refer to the official SPDR website.</p>

</p>

### JSPS Research Fellowship for Young Scientists (Employed at RIKEN)

<p class="is-indent">Similar to the SPDR program, the JSPS Research Fellowship (PD/RPD) hosted by RIKEN supports researchers in independently pursuing projects based on their own research proposals. As fellows are employed by RIKEN, information regarding employment conditions and application procedures can be found on the RIKEN JSPS Fellowship website.
Prospective applicants should contact the laboratory head at an early stage and obtain approval by March of the application year. A formal acceptance document must be prepared, and approval from the Center Director or the President of RIKEN is required. After receiving approval, applicants can obtain an ID and password for the JSPS “Electronic Application System for Research Fellowships” and proceed with the submission of their application materials.</p>

<p class="is-indent">
  <a href="https://www.jsps.go.jp/english/e-pd/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">https://www.jsps.go.jp/english/e-pd/</a>
</p>

### JSPS Postdoctoral Fellowship for Research in Japan

<p class="is-indent">This fellowship provides outstanding early-career researchers from overseas—both before and shortly after completing their Ph.D.—with the opportunity to conduct collaborative research under the guidance of a host researcher in Japan. The fellowship period is up to two years. Eligible applicants must hold the nationality of a country with diplomatic relations with Japan, possess a Ph.D. degree, and have obtained their Ph.D. within six years as of April 1 of the appointment year. There are typically two application cycles each year (around the end of April and the end of August). Please note that RIKEN’s internal deadlines are one month earlier (around the end of March and the end of July), so careful attention to the schedule is required. Prospective applicants should contact the laboratory head well in advance of the internal deadlines.</p>
